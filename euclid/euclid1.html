Proposition 32 proves that the sum of the angles in a triangle is 180 degrees or in Euclid's eloquent words, "In any triangle, if one of the sides is produced, then the exterior angle equals the sum of the two interior and opposite angles, and the sum of the three interior angles of the triangle equals two right angles."
